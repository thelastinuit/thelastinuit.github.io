To Love You

I'm not a poet, nor an artist true,
I'm only in love with you, my dear.
In awe of all your ways, your being too,
Please go easy on me, keep me near.

These are my thoughts and feelings, pure and new,
Of something I've never felt before.
You are outstanding, like the morning dew,
Please forgive my past, let love restore.

If I've erred, let mercy be our guide,
I'm yours eternally, if you so wish.
In you, my heart and soul will confide,
Together, our love's light will never diminish.

So here I stand, with all my heart and plea,
Love you, endlessly.

I've been thinking, God knows it's been days,
A torment within, my mind in a maze.
I've been torturing myself, in a daze,
Seeking the light in these shadowed ways.

I don't have an answer, my heart's refrain,
Why love's profound, we may never explain.
It's beyond our grasp, this tender pain,
A mystery held in love's gentle chain.

Should we seek reasons, the why and the how,
Or simply accept love's uncharted course?
In its pure essence, no need to avow,
For love's true power, we cannot enforce.

If not notwithstanding, let love be free,
A boundless force, an eternal decree.

You said I disgust you, I cannot blame,
How else could you feel, if my father spurned?
Pushed me away, left us both to claim,
Rejection's shadow, in hearts we've discerned.

I was just a baby, mom a young lady,
Both felt abandoned in our own sad way.
Love is a mystery, not always steady,
A force beyond control, come what may.

Yet I'm glad it's you who I deeply love,
Despite the trials, despite all I've done.
In your presence, I find light from above,
Your love is a battle that's already won.

Though it may be over, perhaps we're through,
In my heart's depths, I'll always love you.
